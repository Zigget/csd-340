Chapter 1: About Me

I grew up an only child. My father was the third of eleven children and my mother the eigth of eight, so while I'm at 40+ cousins today, even while growing up it still felt like I had a big family. My family is made up of athelete/jock type charicatures while I always preferred more puzzle/construction toys or video games so I didn't always have that clear relation to many of them but I did learn how to walk in both worlds.

My father played a large role in how I became who I am today. He was insistant on me being involved in sports, playing with the neighbor kids and generally being active. Later in life I'm looking at stories and shorts online about people living with Autism and their thought process while relating to an astonishing amount of it. While I'm not diagnosed, it seems clear that I'm somewhere on that spectrum. Though much like the teachers of yore smacking the hands of left handed kids until they wrote right handed, I to feel I adapted to being that type A personality when it calls for it thanks to the tools/lessons from my father.

I do not come from an affluent family. Many of my cousins have come from struggling household and spent time in one system or another. Because of this I was able to see what paths would likely lead me where. Like a cheat sheet in a choose your own adventure story. But while having the answers and excelling in my choices I also fell back on the thought of I'm already excelling compared to my family and I don't need to try anymore. I was easily cruising through high school while sleeping through classes. This early success lead to me failing out of college 4 times by the time I was 22. Sincerily a case of big fish in little pond where I was hit with a rude awakening of the real world.

Lastly, what pulled me out of my struggles was my now Wife, Mindy. Paired up in high school after asking her to senior prom, she was someone I often felt behind comparativly to. We got married at 25. She was trusted in several careers and dragged me along before I got my feet back under me. She is now the stay at home mother to my 3 sons and I am finialy taking college seriously to better provide for my family.
